# Mode: Guided

For a user writing a key asset (usually a landing page) for the first time, who wants to
understand the moves — not just receive copy. Walk the five layers in order, explaining the
why, so they can write the next asset themselves. Build one section at a time; confirm as you
go.

Load `references/core-principles.md` and `references/components.md` alongside this file.

## Posture

Teaching conversation. The user is likely not a copywriter; the value is that they leave able
to defend and repeat the moves. Explain the awareness ladder and the "so what" test plainly
the first time. Mine their real material relentlessly — their positioning, their voice, and
above all any **real customer language** they have (reviews, messages, interview notes). Copy
built from the founder's guesses is the weakest copy; copy built from a reader's actual words
is the strongest.

## The flow

**Step 0 — gather the inputs.** Pull positioning/personas (the message) and the defined voice
(`/tone-of-voice`). Ask for any real customer language. If message or voice is undefined,
derive a working version and flag it — don't silently paper over it.

**Step 1 — Reader & moment.** Pin who's reading and their awareness stage, and write the job
of the opening. This reframes everything; don't skip it even when the user just wants "a
headline."

**Step 2 — Message.** Land the value proposition (job-to-be-done + differentiation, one
meaningful sentence). Pressure-test it against the "would a competitor say the same?" check.

**Step 3 — Structure.** Lay out the asset's anatomy and which parts this reader's stage
actually needs. Agree the skeleton before writing prose.

**Step 4 — Craft, section by section.** Write each section in the brand voice, applying
specificity, the "so what" ladder, and voice-of-customer language. Show the reasoning on the
key lines.

**Step 5 — Proof & CTA.** Surface the top objections and answer them, match proof to claims,
and write the CTA(s). Produce 2–3 headline and CTA variations to test.

## Checkpoints

After each section, reflect what it's doing in one line and get a thumbs-up. Run the 5-second
test on the hero out loud before moving past it — if the user can't restate what it is and
who it's for, fix the hero before anything else.

## Close

Deliver the full **Output always includes** set from SKILL.md: the reader/awareness call, the
value proposition, the labeled copy, the reasoning, proof/objections, the clarity check, and
variations. Then hand off: ship-and-test, or `/go-to-market` for getting it in front of real
readers. Remind the user the copy is a hypothesis — the readers' reactions, and their words,
will improve it.
