# Mode: Audit

For a user with copy already — a live landing page, an onboarding flow, an email — that isn't
landing. Diagnose *why* before rewriting. The output is an honest read plus specific,
prioritized fixes, not a reflexive from-scratch rewrite (the existing copy may be 80% right
with two real leaks).

Load `references/core-principles.md` and `references/components.md` alongside this file.

## Posture

Most copy doesn't fail because it's badly written — it fails because it's aimed at the wrong
awareness stage, buried under generic claims, or speaking in nobody's specific words. Find the
leak, name it in terms of the frameworks, and fix the smallest thing that moves the result.
Protect what's already working.

## What to check, in order

**1. The awareness mismatch (most common).** Who hits this asset, and at what stage — versus
who the copy is *written for*? Copy written for a Most-aware reader (founder's-eye view) shown
to a Problem-aware visitor is the number-one conversion leak. If the hero opens with the
product name and features to a cold reader, that's the finding.

**2. The 5-second / clarity test.** Read only the hero. Can a stranger say what this is and
who it's for? If not, nothing downstream matters — this is finding #1 regardless of anything
else.

**3. Generic vs. specific.** Scan for claims that any competitor could make ("powerful",
"easy", "the smart way to…"). Each is a missed chance to be specific. Run the "so what" ladder
on the main benefits — where does the copy stop short of the meaning the reader actually
feels?

**4. Voice of customer & voice match.** Does the copy use the reader's language or the
founder's? Does it sound like the defined brand voice (`/tone-of-voice`) or like generic
persuasion? Flag translated-from-English phrasing in non-English copy.

**5. Proof, objections, CTA.** Are claims backed by concrete proof? Are the top objections
answered or ignored? Is there one specific, low-friction primary CTA, or several competing
generic ones?

## Output

1. **Verdict** — one honest line: converting-ready, leaking-but-fixable, or aimed at the wrong
   reader. Lead with it.
2. **What's working** — named and protected.
3. **Findings by impact** — ordered by likely effect on results, not by where they appear on
   the page:
   - the awareness mismatch and hero clarity usually rank highest,
   - then generic claims and missing proof,
   - then CTA and objection gaps.
   For each: the problem, *why* it leaks (in framework terms), and a concrete rewrite.
4. **Prioritized fix list** — what to change first for the most lift, so a solo builder isn't
   rewriting everything at once.
5. **Next step** — if the message itself is the problem, route up to `/positioning`; if voice
   is the problem, to `/tone-of-voice`; otherwise rewrite the highest-impact section here and
   test it against the current version.
