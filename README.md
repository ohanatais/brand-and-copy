# brand-and-copy

> Your product's brand isn't decoration. It's the first decision your user makes about whether to trust you.

Part of the [build-from-zero](https://github.com/ohanatais/build-from-zero) ecosystem.

Most solo builders treat brand and copy as the last step — something to polish after the product works. That's backwards. The words you use, the colors you choose, and the tone you speak in are all part of the product. They shape whether someone understands what you built, feels like it's for them, and decides to stay.

This repo covers everything between "I know what I'm building" and "I'm ready to write code" — the layer that makes your product feel like something, not just do something.

---

## What's inside

### Brand identity
How to create a visual identity for your product without a designer.

You don't need an agency. You need clarity about what your product stands for — and a consistent visual system that communicates that without you being in the room to explain it.

- How to define the personality of your product before touching any tool
- Color: how to choose a palette with intention, not just taste
- Typography: what makes a font choice feel right for a digital product
- Logo: how to create something simple that actually works at small sizes
- How to document your visual identity so Claude, a designer, or future-you can use it consistently

### Tone of voice
How to define how your product talks — and make sure it always sounds like itself.

Tone of voice isn't about being funny or formal. It's about being consistent. A product that sounds different on the landing page, in the onboarding, and in error messages feels unfinished — even if the code is perfect.

- The difference between brand voice (who you are) and tone (how you adjust by context)
- How to define your voice with adjectives that actually constrain your writing
- The "say it three ways" exercise: right, wrong, and on the edge
- Words and phrases to use — and words that break the voice
- How to write error messages, empty states, and microcopy that stay on brand

### Product copy
How to write the words that convert — landing page, onboarding, and beyond.

Copy isn't about sounding smart. It's about making the right person, in the right moment, feel like this product was made for them. Generic copy is invisible. Specific copy converts.

- The anatomy of a landing page that works
- How to write a headline that does actual work (not just sounds good)
- Value proposition: how to say what you do in one sentence that means something
- How to write a CTA that feels inevitable, not pushy
- How to handle objections without sounding defensive
- Onboarding copy: the first five minutes that determine whether someone stays

---

## The relationship between these three

They're not independent pieces. They compound.

Your **brand identity** sets the visual container. Your **tone of voice** sets the verbal personality. Your **product copy** is where both come alive in the moments that matter — when someone lands on your page, reads your first email, or hits an error for the first time.

Build them in that order. Copy written without a defined tone sounds like everyone else. A tone defined without a brand identity has nothing to anchor to.

**Claude Code skills** for each stage live directly in this repo — one folder per topic, each with its own `SKILL.md`, `references/`, and `README.md`. Install them in your project to use these frameworks directly inside Claude Code.

- [`brand-identity`](brand-identity) — create a visual + verbal identity without a designer, derived from what the brand stands for
- [`tone-of-voice`](tone-of-voice) — define how the product talks, and keep it sounding like itself across every surface
- [`product-copy`](product-copy) — write the words that convert: landing page, onboarding, microcopy
